📅 How to Plan

Planning isn’t about making pretty calendars. It’s about making space for the life (and grades) you want — and avoiding the panic spiral.

1. Start With Purpose

Before anything else, ask yourself why you’re doing this degree. You don’t need a perfect answer. You just need a direction.

  • Are you drawn to AI, software engineering, security, or something else?
  • Do you like building things, solving theory problems, or launching ideas?
  • What kind of career—or lifestyle—are you aiming for by graduation?

This shapes how you prioritize courses, electives, internships, and projects.

2. Know What You’re Signing Up For

A full-time CS degree at VU expects ~40 hours/week. That includes:

  • Lectures and tutorials
  • Self-study
  • Labs and assignments
  • Exam prep

If you're working a side job or have other responsibilities, plan accordingly. Your time isn’t infinite.

Just make sure you get 42 credits.

3. Track Before You Plan

Track your time for one week before you make a plan:

  • Log every hour (sleep, study, social, Netflix... all of it)
  • Use Notion, Google Sheets, or a paper planner

You can’t fix what you don’t measure.

4. Prioritize Like an Adult (Eisenhower Matrix)

Most CS work is “Important but Not Urgent.” That’s where the growth happens. Don’t live in panic mode.

5. Build a Real Planning System

📅 Weekly Plan: Every Sunday, block lectures, 2–3 hrs of self-study, assignments, review, breaks, and rest. Leave buffer space.

📆 Semester Overview: Map all deadlines and exams. Break each course into weekly chunks. Work backwards.

6. Estimate Time Realistically

If you think a task will take 1 hour, plan for 1.5–2. Most students overestimate their focus.

7. Plan All Areas of Your Life

CS is demanding. But you're not a machine. Plan time for work, rest, sleep, workouts, meals, and doing nothing.

8. Review Weekly

Planning isn’t one-and-done. Every week: what worked, what didn’t, what’s changing. Then adjust.

9. If Planning Fails

  • Too ambitious → cut scope, do fewer things better
  • Always procrastinating → use Pomodoro, block distractions
  • No motivation → revisit your purpose, talk to a peer or advisor
  • Mental health low → seek support early (VU psychologists exist!)

10. Tools That Help

  • Google Calendar / Outlook – best for fixed time blocks
  • Notion / Trello – visual task trackers
  • Forest / Pomofocus – Pomodoro apps for focus
  • Paper planner – still works if you’re more analog

📖 How to Read Smart

Skim first. Then question. Then deep dive. Don’t read to memorize — read to find what’s worth remembering.

🧠 Processing Info

Use outlines, diagrams, or even voice notes. Don’t just re-read. Turn material into something new so your brain does the work.

💡 Remembering Long-Term

Use spaced repetition: Review after 1 hour, 1 day, 1 week, 1 month. Make mistakes while recalling — it’s what builds memory.

🚨 The BSA Explained

  • You need at least 42 EC in Year 1.
  • If you don’t make it, you may get a negative BSA and be barred from continuing.
  • If you have special circumstances, talk to your academic advisor early.

💬 Real Advice No One Tells You

  • Start DSA in week 1. Not later. Seriously.
  • You won’t understand everything. That’s okay — ask anyway.
  • 2 friends per course > 100 lecture slides alone.
  • Your goal isn’t perfect notes. It’s passing and learning what matters.
